Ripple and Surge

Elevating user trust with a polished web presence

Context

Ripple and Surge is a personal training company that empowers sedentary professional men to lead healthier, happier, lives.

I was hired as a UX Designer to expand their customer reach beyond Instagram by redesigning their existing website to increase conversion rates.

Duration

June 2023 - August 2023 (3 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Iteration, Branding

Tools

Figma, Photoshop

Getting the Ball Rolling

I met with the Owner and Social Media Manager of Ripple and Surge to understand the scope of the website redesign and the brand's identity. This helped me establish a creative direction that would resonate with their target audience and a checkpoint cadence to keep the company in the loop on my progress.

Forming a Game Plan

I then audited the existing site to identify and prioritize improvements. Then, I conducted moderated usability studies with individuals who matched the company’s key demographic. From my findings, I uncovered the following pain points:

Low Visual Appeal

A lack of adherence to visual design principles and a failure to employ best practices for responsive design resulted in low content readability and findability.

Unintuitive Booking Flow

Users found the website’s navigation to be confusing and frustrating. This was due to a mismatch between their mental models and the site’s navigation structure.

User Distrust

Users were reluctant to engage with Ripple and Surge as they felt there was a lack of professionalism and brand consistency across platforms. Some questioned whether they were even interacting with the same company they saw on Instagram.

Sizing Up the Competition

To refine my perspective on design trends and incorporate industry best practices, I audited competitors’ sites. My key takeaways to incorporate were:

1
Clear labels and headers to improve discoverability of content
2
Engaging visuals that reinforced the brand’s identity and highlighted content
3
Abundant social proof (i.e. before/after pictures, testimonials, reviews, press release links etc.)
Down to the Wire

I began the redesign process by sketching to visualize the new information structure for the desktop site. As part of my goal to improve conversion rates, I also explored alternative methods for users to book consultation calls.

After translating my sketches to digital wireframes, I solicited feedback from Ripple and Surge.
