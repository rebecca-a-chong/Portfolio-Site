Ripple and Surge

Improving conversion rates through a responsive redesign

Context

Ripple and Surge, a personal training company, empowers sedentary professional men to lead healthier, happier, lives.

I worked as the sole UX Designer tasked with redesigning their website to extend their customer reach beyond Instagram and boost conversion rates.

Duration

June 2023 - August 2023 (3 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Iteration, Branding

Tools

Figma, Photoshop

Getting the Ball Rolling

I aligned with the company's Owner and Social Media Manager on the scope of the redesign and the brand's identity. The goal was to establish a creative direction that would resonate with their target audience and create a touchpoint cadence to keep the company informed on my progress.

Forming a Game Plan

I identified and prioritized improvements by conducting a website audit and usability studies with individuals who represented the company's target demographic. 3 main areas of focus emerged:

Visual Design

The site lacked adherence to design principles and responsive design best practices, resulting in low content readability and findability.

The Booking Process

Due to a mismatch between users' mental models and the site’s navigation structure, users were confused and frustrated when tasked with booking an appointment.

Brand Consistency

Users hesitated to engage with the Ripple and Surge site as they felt there was a lack of professionalism and brand consistency across platforms. Some were skeptical of whether they were interacting with the same company they saw on Instagram.

Sizing Up the Competition

Armed with an understanding of "what" to improve, I delved into the "how." Analyzing other trainers' websites revealed industry trends and best practices:

1
Flat site hierarchies with clear navigation titles and content headers made discovering content easy
2

The use of brand-consistent visuals and engaging animations both reinforced brand identity and subtly drew a user's attention to content

3
Including abundant social proof (i.e. before/after pictures, testimonials, reviews, press release links etc.) can increase a user's confidence in a trainer's skills
Core Features

The site evolved steadily through 3 revisions, culminating in a final design hand-off to Ripple and Surge for development. Key features of the new site included:

A Visual Overhaul

To enhance brand trust, I added social proof elements such as client testimonials and Google reviews. I also embedded the company's Instagram feed to further strengthen the connection between the site and their social media presence.

To tie everything together, I incorporated motion and created brand-consistent visual elements reflecting the company’s voice and values.

Streamlined Booking

In addition to a new sitemap with improved navigation, I took a 3-pronged approach to revising the booking experience.

My efforts led to business-side efficiencies by reducing time-intensive email exchanges stemming from users neglecting to specify their availability and/or desired service in the original web form’s open-ended message box.

Dedicated Mobile Browsing Experience

I tailored the layout for mobile devices to increase the site's reach, providing a user-friendly browsing experience irrespective of a user's device.

Final Thoughts

Working with my first freelance client was a lesson in navigating the nuanced relationship between designer expertise and client preferences. When my design ideas were met with hesitancy, I embraced these moments as opportunities to strengthen my ability to articulate my design rationale. By crafting a persuasive narrative using before-and-after visuals and research insights, I was able to generate buy-in.

Given the chance to revisit this project, I'd be interested in leveraging analytics tools to examine metrics such as the bounce rate, scroll depth, and time on page to more deeply assess the effectiveness of the content's final layout.
