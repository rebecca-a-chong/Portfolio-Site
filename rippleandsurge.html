Ripple and Surge

Improving brand awareness with a cross-platform design

Context

Ripple and Surge, a personal training company, empowers sedentary professional men to lead healthier, happier, lives.

I worked as the sole UX Designer tasked with redesigning their website to extend their customer reach beyond Instagram and boost brand awareness.

Outcome

Post-redesign, average site traffic increased from 0 to 30 visitors per month.

Duration

June 2023 - August 2023 (3 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Iteration, Branding

Tools

Figma, Photoshop

Getting the Ball Rolling

In a saturated market, being easily identifiable can mean the difference between a customer sign-up or being overlooked.

I collaborated closely with the company's Owner and Social Media Manager to define the brand's identity and scope of the redesign to establish a creative direction that would resonate with their target audience.

Forming a Game Plan

I identified and prioritized improvements by conducting a website audit and usability studies with individuals who represented the company's target demographic. 3 main areas of focus emerged:

Visual Design

The site lacked adherence to design principles and responsive design best practices, resulting in low content readability and findability.

The Booking Process

Due to a mismatch between users' mental models and the site’s navigation structure, users were confused and frustrated when tasked with booking an appointment.

Brand Consistency

Users hesitated to engage with the Ripple and Surge site as they felt there was a lack of professionalism and brand consistency across platforms.

Sizing Up the Competition

Armed with an understanding of "what" to improve, I delved into the "how." Analyzing other trainers' websites revealed industry trends and best practices:

1
Flatgi site hierarchies with clear navigation titles and content headers made discovering content easy
2

The use of brand-consistent visuals and suble animations both reinforced brand identity and delicately drew a user's attention to content

3
Including abundant social proof (i.e. before/after pictures, testimonials, reviews, press release links etc.) can increase a user's confidence in a trainer's skills
The Final Design

The site evolved steadily through 3 revisions, culminating in a final design hand-off to Ripple and Surge for development. Below are the key features of the new site.

A Visual Overhaul

To enhance brand trust, I added social proof elements such as client testimonials and Google reviews. I also embedded the company's Instagram feed to further strengthen the connection between the site and their social media presence.

To tie everything together, I incorporated motion and created brand-consistent visual elements reflecting the company’s voice and values.

Dedicated Mobile Browsing Experience

I tailored the layout for mobile devices to increase the site's reach, providing a user-friendly browsing experience irrespective of a user's device.

Streamlined Booking

In addition to a new sitemap with improved navigation, I took a 3-pronged approach to revising the booking experience.

My efforts led to business-side efficiencies by reducing time-intensive email exchanges stemming from users neglecting to specify their availability and/or desired service in the original web form’s open-ended message box.

Final Thoughts

Working with my first freelance client was a lesson in navigating the nuanced relationship between designer expertise and client preferences. When my design suggestions were met with hesitancy, I embraced these moments as opportunities to strengthen my ability to articulate my design rationale. By crafting a persuasive narrative using before-and-after visuals and research insights, I was able to generate buy-in.

Given the chance to revisit this project, I'd be interested to more deeply explore user behavior by moving beyond usability tests. I'd like to leverage analytics tools to examine metrics such as the bounce rate, heatmaps, scroll depth, and time on page to assess the effectiveness of the site's information hierarchy and inform future design iterations.
