Pivot

Connecting career changers to real info from real people

Context

Pivot is a conceptual mobile app that leverages the job history and aspirations of career changers to provide them with the knowledge needed to make informed career decisions.

Duration

January 2023 - April 2023 (4 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing, Design Iteration

Tools

Figma

The Origin Story

Inspired by these findings and my own career journey, I set out to design a solution that could help others navigate their career transitions with greater confidence.

The Problem Space

I reviewed existing literature on employment trends to gain a solid grasp of the domain, then conducted 1:1 interviews with users who were considering or had successfully changed careers to understand their motivations, emotions, and decision-making process.

I uncovered 3 significant insights:

1
Career changers spend a significant amount of time searching for and verifying the credibility of information across scattered sources.
2
Career changers prioritize success stories from people with similar career journeys over other generic content.
3
Being able to immerse themselves in the day-to-day experiences of a potential job was critical for a person to confidently commit to a career change.

Guided by this question, I decided that my solution should:

Bringing it to Life

I explored different layout ideas by sketching paper wireframes. Afterwards, I reviewed my drawings and incorporated the parts I liked best into low-fi digital wireframes using Figma.

Before moving forward with the high-fi version, I conducted two rounds of usability testing and incorporated user feedback. I also created a style guide to maintain consistency across each frame.

The Final Design

With the designs finalized, I am proud to show the key features and highlights of the app below.

Sign Up

The Sign Up flow is split between Pivot’s two user groups: Pivoters and Experts.

After entering some basic information, Pivoters can opt to add details about their professional background and interests to see more relevant content on their homepage.

Search

In addition to accessing curated content from the homepage, users can utilize the Search Bar to explore using keywords. Results can be fine-tuned with a variety of filters.

Expert Profiles

Expert profiles convey credibility at a glance with a concise summary of their credentials and a prominent blue Verified icon, signifying that their professional background has been authenticated. Pivoters can toggle an expandable accordion to view additional details if desired.

Ask a Question

Pivoters can pose personalized questions to Experts of their choice. To set expectations regarding response rates, a metric indicating the likelihood of receiving a response is displayed. When an answer is received, Pivoters will receive a notification with a link to the clip containing their answer.

This feature creates a positive content feedback loop as Pivoters will be able to receive answers to their most pressing questions while Experts receive inspiration for their next upload.

Leave a Tip

After viewing an Expert’s response, users can express their gratitude by leaving a tip if they found the answer satisfactory. Pivot will prompt users to link a credit card or a PayPal account to process the transaction if payment details have not been previously entered.

The tipping function benefits both user groups as it ensures Pivoters will receive high-quality answers and provides Experts with a compelling incentive for participating in the platform.

Final Thoughts

Although this was an academic project, I treated Pivot as a genuine, market-ready product, incorporating features to ensure sustained engagement from both Pivoters and Experts. In particular, I explored monetization avenues in search of an equilibrium between a user’s access to valuable information and the app's financial viability. After careful consideration of several options, I ultimately decided on a tipping feature, directing 100% of contributions to Experts. This choice was made to strategically reach a critical mass of users, therefore enhancing future ad revenue projections. Overall, it was interesting to observe just how much of an influence a business’ goals had on potential solutions.

A future opportunity I would like to explore further is Experts’ view of the app. As a spreadsheet/dashboard fanatic, I would love to visualize how Experts could track their audience engagement with metrics such as watch time, re-watches, follower growth etc.
