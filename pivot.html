Pivot

Connecting career changers to real info from real people

Context

Pivot is a conceptual mobile app that leverages the job history and aspirations of career changers to provide them with the knowledge needed to make informed career decisions.

Duration

January 2023 - April 2023 (4 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing, Design Iteration

Tools

Figma

The Origin Story

Answering the question “What do I want to do in life?” is a daunting task and it’s common for this answer to change. In fact, in a survey conducted by Indeed’s Editorial Team,

49% of respondents had made a dramatic career shift, with 88% of them ultimately happier since making the change and

65% of respondents who hadn’t changed careers indicated that they were currently thinking about, or had previously considered, switching.

Inspired by these results and my personal career journey, I aimed to design a solution that could help others navigate their career transition with confidence.

The Problem Space

I reviewed existing literature on employment trends to gain a solid grasp of the domain, then conducted one-on-one interviews with users who were considering or had successfully changed careers to delve deeper into the insights I uncovered.

Using my research findings, I created a persona and journey map to better visualize my target audience and the opportunities for improvement.



Ideating

Mapping Jessie’s journey made it clear that intervening during the Gather Information stage would have the greatest impact on alleviating her frustrations.

Through my research, I identified three key findings regarding this stage:

1
Career changers spent a significant amount of time searching for and verifying the credibility of information across scattered sources.
2
Career changers prioritized success stories from people with similar career journeys over other generic content.
3
Being able to immerse themselves in the day-to-day experiences of a potential job was critical for instilling confidence in career changers to commit to a change.


These insights were valuable as they helped me conceptualize my solution. From understanding career changers’ perspectives, I knew that my solution should ultimately:

Be a Content Hub. The app should consolidate content from credible career change experts into a single location.

Provide Tailored Information. The app should frontload relevant content that is specific to a user’s unique career journey.

Embrace Realism. The app should present information in a format that mimics real life as closely as possible.

Lo-Fi Drafts
I created paper wireframes. Afterwards, I reviewed my sketches and incorporated the parts I liked best into digital wireframes using Figma.
Styling the Hi-Fi

As I moved forward with designing the high-fidelity version, I created a style guide to main consistency across each frame.

I chose Inter as the typeface for its readability and versatility, as well as its modern appearance. The primary brand color, blue, was selected to convey professionalism, trust, and knowledge, which are all qualities that were highly valued by users.

The Final Design

After conducting two rounds of usability testing and incorporating user feedback, I finalized the designs for Pivot.

Sign Up

The Sign Up flow is split between Pivot’s two user groups: Pivoters and Experts.

New Pivoters can create an account by entering basic information. Opting to add details about their professional background and interests will allow them to see more relevant content on their homepage.

Search

In addition to accessing curated content from the homepage, users can utilize the Search Bar to explore using keywords. Results can be fine-tuned with a variety of filters.

Expert Profiles

Expert profiles convey credibility at a glance with a concise summary of their credentials and a prominent blue Verified icon, signifying that the Pivot team has authenticated their background. Pivoters can delve further by toggling an expandable accordion to view additional details.

Selecting any of the clip thumbnails further down the profile page allows Pivoters to engage with the video player where they can like/dislike clips to see/hide similar content. They can also leave comments, fostering community interaction.

Ask a Question

Pivoters can pose personalized questions to Experts of their choice. To set expectations regarding response rates, a metric indicating the likelihood of receiving a response is displayed below the “Ask a Question” button. When an answer is received, Pivoters will receive a notification with a link to the clip containing their answer.

This feature creates a positive content feedback loop as Pivoters will be able to receive answers to their most pressing questions while Experts receive inspiration for their next upload.

Leave a Tip

After watching an Expert’s response, users have the option to express their gratitude by leaving a tip if they found the answer satisfactory. Pivot will prompt users to link a credit card or a PayPal account to process the transaction if payment details have not been previously entered.

The tipping function benefits both user groups as it ensures Pivoters will receive high-quality answers and provides Experts with a compelling incentive for participating in the platform.
