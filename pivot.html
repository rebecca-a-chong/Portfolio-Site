Pivot

Connecting career changers to real info from real people

Context

Pivot is a conceptual mobile app that leverages the job history and aspirations of career changers to provide them with the knowledge needed to make informed career decisions.

Duration

January 2023 - April 2023 (4 months)

My Role

UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing, Design Iteration

Tools

Figma

Inspired by these findings and my own career journey, I set out to design a solution to help others navigate their career transition with greater confidence.

The Problem Space

I delved into existing literature to gain a solid grasp of the domain, then conducted 1:1 interviews with users who were considering or had successfully changed careers to understand their motivations, emotions, and decision-making process.

I uncovered 3 significant insights:

1
Career changers spend significant time searching for and verifying the credibility of information across scattered sources.
2
They prioritize content from people with similar career journeys over other generic material.
3
Immersing themselves in the day-to-day experiences of a potential job was critical for instilling confidence and commitment in the decision-making process.
Defining A Solution

With a firm understanding of the problem, I began ideating. I asked myself:

I decided that my solution should ultimately:

Centralize Content: The app should be a hub housing content from credible career change experts into a single location.

Tailor Information: The app should consider a user's unique career journey then prioritize relevant content aligned with their trajectory.

Be Video-centric: The app should present information in as realistic a format as possible to offer users an experience that closely mirrors the realities of various jobs.

The Final Design

With the design finalized, I am proud to show the app's key features below!

Sign Up

The Sign Up flow is split between Pivot’s two user groups: Pivoters and Experts.

After entering some basic information, Pivoters can add details about their professional background and interests to see relevant content on their homepage.

Search

Users can access the Search Bar to explore using keywords. Results can be fine-tuned with a variety of filters to help them easily locate the right information.

Expert Profiles

Expert profiles convey credibility at a glance with a concise summary of their credentials and a blue Verified icon, signifying that their professional background has been authenticated. Curious Pivoters can toggle an expandable accordion to view additional details.

Ask a Question

Pivoters can ask custom questions to Experts. When they receive an answer, they'll be notified with a link to the right clip.

This feature creates a positive feedback loop for content as Pivoters can receive answers to their most pressing questions while Experts can receive inspiration for their next upload.

Leave a Tip

After viewing an Expert’s response, users can express their gratitude by choosing to leave a tip.

The tipping function benefits both user groups as it ensures Pivoters will receive high-quality answers and provides Experts with a compelling incentive for participating in the platform.

Final Thoughts

Although this was an academic project, I treated Pivot as a genuine product by exploring monetization avenues in search of an equilibrium between a user’s access to valuable information and the app's financial viability. After carefully weighing several options, I ultimately chose a tipping feature, directing 100% of contributions to Experts. This choice was made to strategically reach a critical mass of users quickly, thereby enhancing future ad revenue projections. Overall, it was intriguing to see how much of an influence a business’ goals plays on selecting a solution.

A future opportunity I would like to explore further is Experts’ view of the app. As a spreadsheet/dashboard fanatic, I would love to visualize how Experts could track their audience engagement with metrics such as watch time, re-watches, follower growth etc.
